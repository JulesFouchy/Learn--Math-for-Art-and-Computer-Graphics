sin(x)/x​

Take 5 to 10 minutes to try and graph sin⁡(x)x\frac{\sin(x)}{x}xsin(x)​.

Then verify you drawing with a graphing tool like Desmos.

Did you get it right ? Or are some parts completely off ? If so, can you imagine why they are the way they are ?

Solution​

First sin⁡(x)\sin(x)sin(x) varies between −1-1−1 and 111, and therefore sin⁡(x)×1x\sin(x)\times\frac{1}{x}sin(x)×x1​ varies between −1x-\frac{1}{x}−x1​ and 1x\frac{1}{x}x1​. Our graph will look like the graph of sin⁡\sinsin, but it will be bounded by the graphs of −1x-\frac{1}{x}−x1​ and 1x\frac{1}{x}x1​.

Then, around 0 : sin(x)sin(x)sin(x) and xxx are very similar (we say that they are equivalent and we write it sin⁡(x)∼x\sin(x) \sim xsin(x)∼x)

And since dividing a number by itself gives 111, sin⁡(x)x≈1\frac{\sin(x)}{x} \approx 1xsin(x)​≈1 as we get close to 0.

One objection might be that we are dividing by xxx which is almost 000, so the result should tend to infinity ; but actually since sin⁡(x)\sin(x)sin(x) is also very close to 0, they compensate each other and the result tends to 1 because sin⁡(x)≈x\sin(x) \approx xsin(x)≈x.
